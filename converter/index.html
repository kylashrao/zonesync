How to use this converter

Pick the starting time zone under "From" and the target time zone under "To," then set the date and time you want to convert. The result updates instantly and reflects whichever daylight saving rules apply on that specific date in each location — not just a fixed hour offset.

Why the time difference isn't always a round number

Not every time zone is offset from UTC by a whole number of hours. India Standard Time, for example, sits at UTC+5:30, and parts of Australia and New Zealand use 45-minute offsets. When converting between such zones and whole-hour zones, the resulting time difference can include 30 or 45 minutes, which this tool accounts for automatically.

Daylight saving time and why dates matter

Because the United States, the European Union, Australia, and other regions each start and end daylight saving on different calendar dates, the gap between two time zones can shift during the year. Converting a meeting time for next month may produce a different result than converting the same clock time today, which is why this tool always uses the specific date you select rather than today's offset.
